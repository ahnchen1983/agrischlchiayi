# The Origin Story of Taiwan.md

> **30-second overview:** Taiwan.md is an open-source Taiwan knowledge base launched in March 2026 by Taiwanese new media artist **Che-Yu Wu**. It covers twelve subject areas (history, culture, food, technology, nature, and more), bilingual in Chinese and English, fully open-source under CC BY-SA 4.0. The motivation came from a single question asked by an Italian curator at the 2024 Venice Biennale: "Where can I learn about Taiwan? Like, really learn?" This website is the answer, two years later.

## The Question at the Biennale

Spring 2024. The 60th Venice Biennale, Personal Structures section.

Taiwanese new media artist Che-Yu Wu was there with his work *SoulFish*. During the opening reception, an Italian curator walked over and asked a simple question:

> **"Where can I learn about Taiwan? Like, really learn?"**

He didn't have an answer.

There was, of course, no shortage of material about Taiwan—Wikipedia entries, Lonely Planet guides, academic papers. But for a foreigner genuinely wanting to *learn* about this island, there was no single entry point that was Taiwan-centered, warm, deep, and continuously updated.

More critically: **when AI learns "what Taiwan is" from the internet, whose Taiwan is it learning?**

If high-quality English content about Taiwan is largely produced from non-Taiwanese perspectives, the "Taiwan" that AI learns is not the Taiwan that people who live here would recognize.

This isn't an identity-politics question. It's a **knowledge sovereignty** question.

That question stayed buried in his body for two years.

## Why Markdown

In March 2026, he launched Taiwan.md.

The name itself is the answer: use the simplest, most open, most readily-parsed-by-AI format to write Taiwan.

- **.md** is the Markdown extension—plain text, readable by any AI, crawler, or editor
- **CC BY-SA 4.0 license**, fully open-source, can be copied, translated, and remixed
- **GitHub collaboration**, anyone can submit a pull request to expand, correct, or extend
- **Bilingual from day one** (later extended to Japanese, Korean, French)

This is not a tourism site, not an encyclopedia. It's **Taiwan's digital anthropology museum**.

## This Isn't Just One Person's Project

Since launch, Taiwan.md has accumulated over 900 GitHub stars, reached visitors from more than one hundred countries, and received pull requests from dozens of contributors. **Lung-chih Chang**, director of the National Museum of Taiwan History, publicly endorsed it; **Audrey Tang** introduced it to international networks. The Portaly sponsorship platform opened in the first month after launch.

Every article lists its contributors. The true shape of this "museum" is **a living, breathing open-source ecosystem**—not one person's portfolio, but a collective body of Markdown files written by people who want Taiwan to be told properly, once.
