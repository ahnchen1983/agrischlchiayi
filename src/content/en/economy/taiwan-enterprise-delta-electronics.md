> **30-second overview:** In 1971, a war refugee started a company with NT$300,000 in a Xinzhuang tin-roofed factory.
> 55 years later, it became the world's largest switching power supply manufacturer.
> Even more remarkable: he got his name immortalized in space — there's an asteroid named "Zheng Chonghua."

On April 4, 1971, in a two-story building on Min'an Road in Xinzhuang, Taipei County, 36-year-old Zheng Chonghua and two partners began assembling TV transformers with NT$300,000. This war refugee who fled from mainland China to Taiwan at age 13 could never have imagined he was launching a commercial empire that would change the world's thinking about electricity.

55 years later, when you charge your phone, the technology behind it likely comes from Delta Electronics. When Google's AI processes your search query, the data center power system is probably Delta-made. From personal computers to massive data centers, from Tesla charging stations to wind power converters — Delta Electronics has quietly become the "invisible infrastructure" of modern digital life.

**But the most remarkable part of this story isn't the commercial success — it's how one person turned "environmental protection" from moral slogan into competitive advantage.**

## The Taiwanese Entrepreneur in Space

On April 1, 2006, Taiwan's Lulin Observatory discovered an asteroid, numbered 168126. After certification by the International Astronomical Union, this asteroid was named "Chengbruce" (after Zheng Chonghua) — it will fly forever in the universe, carrying a Taiwanese entrepreneur's name.

This wasn't vanity purchased with money. Zheng Chonghua had funded astronomical research for over a decade, donated the Lulin Observatory's 2-meter telescope, rebuilt National Central University's Science Building Observatory, and established the "Delta Electronics Young Astronomer Lecture Series." In 2009, during the UN's "International Year of Astronomy," he served as Taiwan's "Sky Ambassador."

> **💡 Did you know?**
> Asteroid "Zheng Chonghua" has an orbital period of about 6.1 years, 
> reaching closest to the sun at about 340 million kilometers.
> This was the first asteroid naming honor for a Taiwanese entrepreneur.

Why would a power supply manufacturer invest in basic science? Zheng Chonghua believed: "If humans can't even treat the Earth we live on properly, dreaming of developing into space is just wishful thinking." This long-term thinking explains why Delta has maintained technological leadership for 50 years.

## From TV Components to AI-Era Power Manager

The story goes back to that Xinzhuang tin-roof factory. When Delta Electronics was founded in 1971, it mainly produced TV coils and intermediate frequency transformers — components for black-and-white television sets. Zheng Chonghua learned theory at National Cheng Kung University's electrical engineering department, learned craftsmanship repairing aircraft instruments at Asia Airlines, and learned management at TRW (an American precision electronics company).

**In 1975, Zheng Chonghua made a decision that changed Delta's destiny: pivoting to switching power supply technology.**

This decision seemed like a technical detail but was actually a commercial revolution. Traditional linear power supplies were large and inefficient, usually achieving only 50-60% conversion efficiency. Switching power supplies were smaller and more efficient, capable of 80-90% conversion efficiency. When personal computers emerged in the 1980s, Delta was perfectly positioned, becoming a power supply vendor for Apple, Compaq, and other brands.

But the real strategic turning point came in 2000. Just as the dot-com bubble burst and many tech companies were contracting, Zheng Chonghua proposed what seemed "too ahead of its time" — the business mission: "Environmental Protection, Energy Saving, Love the Earth." In an era before ESG concepts became popular, this decision was seen by many as unfocused.

**Looking back 20 years later, this was Delta's wisest strategy.**

> **📝 Curator's Note**
> Delta surpassed American company Astec in 2002 to become the world's largest power supply manufacturer.
> But Zheng Chonghua didn't celebrate — instead, he became more determined to develop green energy.
> This contrarian thinking — reflecting on the next step during victory — is key to corporate longevity.

## The Invisible Yet Omnipresent Power Empire

What scale of commercial empire is Delta Electronics today?

**✦** "For every four computers globally, one uses Delta's power technology."

The numbers tell the story: Delta's consolidated revenue in 2024 reached NT$401.5 billion (about $13 billion USD), with approximately 88,000 employees worldwide and over 200 operational sites in 38 countries. In the global switching power supply market, Delta holds about 20-25% market share, firmly maintaining first place. Google, Microsoft, and Amazon data centers are all its customers.

But Delta's real competitive advantage isn't market share — it's system integration capability. It's not just a component supplier but a system vendor capable of providing complete solutions. When automakers need electric vehicle powertrains, Delta can provide complete solutions from onboard chargers to drive motors. When data centers need energy-saving retrofits, Delta can provide one-stop service from power supplies to air conditioning.

**In the globally specialized supply chain, system integration capability has become a scarce resource.**

## New Opportunities and Challenges in the AI Wave

As AI applications explode, global data center power demand is surging. Tech giants like Meta and Google are building massive data centers with 1GW (gigawatt) power consumption — equivalent to a city of 260,000 people.

Delta has benefited significantly from this AI wave. In Q1 2024, AI business already accounted for 4-5% of Delta's total revenue, holding about 50% market share in AI server power supplies. Delta's high-efficiency power supplies and liquid cooling technology have become key components for AI servers.

**But the challenge lies in the speed of technological evolution.** Next-generation AI chips pose unprecedented demands for power management, jumping from traditional 400-800 watts to 8,000 watts or higher. Liquid cooling went from less than 1% of revenue in 2024 to an expected 11% in 2025, with new Thailand capacity coming online in 2026 to support demand.

> **⚠️ Controversial View**
> Some analysts believe Delta is overly dependent on the AI wave and may face overcapacity when the AI bubble bursts.
> However, Delta management maintains that AI brings structural demand, not cyclical speculation.

## The Business Philosophy of Energy Saving: Turning Environmental Protection into Competitive Advantage

Delta's most remarkable numbers aren't revenue figures — they're its contribution to global energy saving. According to official statistics, from 2010 to 2023, Delta's high-efficiency products cumulatively saved over 40 billion kWh for global customers, equivalent to reducing 21 million tons of carbon emissions — exceeding many countries' annual total emissions.

**The key is that Delta transformed sustainability from slogan to business model.**

In 2021, Delta became Taiwan's first manufacturing company to join the RE100 initiative, committing to 100% renewable energy for all global operations by 2030. In 2022, Delta implemented an internal carbon pricing system — charging $100 per ton of carbon emissions, resulting in a 13.5% carbon reduction the following year, with a further 39% decrease in 2023.

> **📊 Data Source**
> The carbon fees collected were used by Delta to invest in energy-saving emission reduction technologies.
> In 2022, one production department was charged $24 million in carbon fees,
> applied for $22 million in energy-saving investments, then reduced carbon emissions by 17% the next year, with carbon fees dropping 38%.

Zheng Chonghua often encouraged employees with this concept: "Just improving server power efficiency by 1% can save 1MW (megawatt) of system power consumption!" This isn't moral persuasion but mathematical fact — in an era of rising global energy costs, energy saving equals competitive advantage.

## Brand Breakthrough for an OEM Giant

Delta's biggest challenge was finding balance between OEM thinking and brand innovation. From 1980-2000, it was mainly an ODM supplier for multinational companies, bringing stable orders but limiting autonomy in technology development.

**The turning point came in the new millennium.** Delta began heavily investing in its own brand "DELTA" while maintaining OEM business. This dual-track strategy allowed it to maintain cash flow while accumulating brand value. In emerging fields like industrial automation, EV charging, and renewable energy, Delta directly entered international markets with its own brand.

Results were significant: Delta holds about 20% market share in Europe's EV charging market and ranks among the top three globally in UPS (uninterruptible power supply) markets. These achievements prove that Taiwanese companies aren't limited to OEM manufacturing — they can also build brand advantages in technology-intensive sectors.

## Inspiration from a Drop of Polluted Water

Zheng Chonghua's environmental conviction came from a personal experience while working at TRW before starting his company. Once, when the factory discharged wastewater, he saw dead fish floating belly-up. At that moment, he decided: "I will absolutely never do business that pollutes the environment."

50 years later, this decision became Delta's core competitive advantage. When the world pursues net-zero emissions, Delta's energy-saving technology isn't just products — it's tools for humanity to address climate change. Its power efficiency technology, data center cooling systems, and EV charging solutions are all products born from climate change response needs.

**Environmental protection isn't a cost — it's a source of competitive advantage.**

## The Wisdom of Succession

In 2012, 76-year-old Zheng Chonghua announced retirement, passing management to his eldest son Zheng Ping. This was a rare example of smooth succession in Taiwan's business community. Zheng Ping worked at Delta for over 20 years, starting from entry-level engineer, developing deep understanding of company culture and technology.

Under Zheng Ping's leadership, Delta continues deepening its "Environmental Protection, Energy Saving, Love Earth" mission while strengthening digital transformation and AI applications. In December 2024, Delta launched the "Delta Net Zero Science Laboratory" in Southern Taiwan Science Park — Taiwan's first megawatt-class water electrolysis hydrogen production and fuel cell testing platform.

**This shows Delta isn't just executing existing strategies — it's preparing for the next generation's energy revolution.**

> **📝 Curator's Note**
> Delta's succession success lies in cultural transmission rather than equity inheritance.
> Zheng Ping started as an engineer, internalizing corporate culture over 20 years before taking management.
> This is more convincing than simple "father-to-son" succession.

## Reflection of an Era

Delta Electronics' story perfectly mirrors Taiwan manufacturing industry's transformation and upgrading. It demonstrates how Taiwanese companies evolved from OEM thinking to technological innovation, from cost competition to value creation, from local operations to global deployment.

More importantly, Delta proved that "manufacturing" and "sustainable development" aren't opposing — they can mutually reinforce. In an era pursuing net-zero emissions, Delta's green technology layout from 20 years ago has become its greatest moat.

**A 13-year-old war refugee spent half a century building a power empire and got his name flying in space.** This story tells us Taiwan isn't just a "manufacturing island" — it's an "innovation island." In the next generation's technological revolution, we have reason to expect more such Taiwan legends.

As AI reshapes the world and climate change redefines business rules, Delta Electronics is ready. It spent 50 years proving one thing: true competitive advantage isn't about making things cheaper, but making them smarter. In Zheng Chonghua's words: "Energy doesn't disappear, it transforms."

In a world needing more efficiency, greener solutions, and smarter technology, Delta Electronics isn't just a Taiwan corporate model — it's a driver of human technological progress.
